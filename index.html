Skills

Product thinking

Digital products are alive even if we know they are not based on organic matter; they are constantly evolving based on the people who work on them and their ability to turn problem insights into products that solve these problems more efficiently to satisfy and delight users and customers.

I leverage all kinds of methods to perform qualitative and quantitative analysis to measure for success.

Interaction design

Digital products must be intuitive, meet the user where and how the user expects them and get out of the way when they are no longer needed.

I use effective tools to convert the product requirements into simple and elegant user interface systems:

  • Mind maps to capture the requirements of stakeholders.
  • Flow chart diagrams to map user flows using Figjam.
  • UI prototype using Figma or Adobe XD.
  • Web UI components using Visual Studio Code + HTML, CSS, JS and React.

Visual design

The first impressions matter, and most of this sentiment is derived from the form and aesthetics of the product.

I use tools to achieve high resolution and detail so that users feel constantly engaged.

  • UI components library using Figma or Adobe XD.
  • Illustration and iconography using Adobe Illustrator and Fresco.
  • Animation and special affects using Adobe After Effects.
  • Photo editing and graphics for print using Adobe Photoshop.

Proactivity

Sometimes problems come knocking at the door, but even in those cases, it’s rarely precisely as the stake-holders describe it; it’s almost like a detective work where there are personas and motivations involved.

I focus on getting to the root of problems to understand the impact, consequences, and motivations.

Intentionality

Genuine respect and kindness are the best way to get your foot into almost anybody’s office. Projects tend to run a lot smoother when the relevant stakeholders are included and communicated about the initiatives and progress.

I go out there and talks to internal and external stake-holders and end-users to get a first-hand understanding of the feasibility of the design options.

Self-awareness

I understand that product design and development is a marathon and not a sprint. Life-Work balance is a priority to create an environment where creativity and productivity can thrive.

Like products, the people behind them are always evolving; the goal is to create a virtuous cycle where talent, health, and peace can expand.
